Hello there! I am a very curious and often distracted person. Nevertheless, I strive to fulfill my role as a planetary geophysicist working toward the exploration—and potentially one day the utilization—of planetary bodies across our Solar System.

I spent most of my childhood playing cricket and gazing at the stars from my village in India. 
Growing up in India, I was fortunate to enjoy a great deal of freedom in my thinking. That freedom allowed me to explore different ideas and perspectives throughout my life. My spiritual journey has taken me through Buddhism, atheism, agnosticism, and eventually back to Hinduism (well, slightly modified one). Alongside my academic journey in Physics and Astrophysics, these experiences have helped me develop an open and adaptable mindset—one that appreciates and respects different cultures, languages, foods, traditions, and ways of thinking.

I am deeply grateful to my parents and to the values of openness and exploration that I experienced while growing up. They encouraged me to ask questions, seek answers for myself, and learn through experience. That curiosity continues to guide both my scientific research and my view of the world today.
